SmokedOut: Android Mobile App University of Notre Dame

Smart Health Class Project

Jake Huber

In the spring semester of my junior year, amidst the coronavirus pandemic, I developed a mobile app called SmokedOut with two team members This project was completed for the Computer Science elective Smart Health that explored how "mobile, wireless, sensing, cloud, and big data technologies can be used to provide significantly improved care to anyone, at anytime, and anywhere, while increasing the coverage, quality, and efficiency of healthcare." (Course Website) Through a semester long project, my group choose to focus on creating a mobile app to help people struggling to quit smoking. The code was all written in Java using Android Studio.

Description


SmokedOut is a mobile app designed to help current smokers break the habit of smoking through creation and tracking of goals, notifications aimed to keep users on track especially at times of the day or week which are known to be challenging, and a social component of the app that holds users accountable in their journey to quitting.
