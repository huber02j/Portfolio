I am currently a junior studying Computer Engineering at the University of Notre Dame with the hope of pursuing a career in software development. I am interested in using computer programming to improve the lives of people often forgotten or overlooked.

This past summer I worked with BOSCO-Uganda – a non-profit NGO in northern Uganda – in their effort to connect people while preserving culture through the implementation of solar power systems, wireless connectivity, and computer centers. While there I led a team of Ugandan interns in creating a data monitoring interface for computer users that can be implemented within refugee camp computer centers. This was developed using Python, Windows PowerShell Process commands, C# Windows Forms, and Microsoft SQL.

When I am not busy working with computers, I enjoy playing soccer for Notre Dame’s Varsity Men’s soccer team as a rostered walk-on. With the little time left I find myself drawn to learning about life’s deeper questions through a Theology minor and investigating intricate international and domestic social issues through seminars with the Center for Social Concerns.

If you are looking for someone who only stops when the job is done, always finds a way, is willing to risk stepping outside of the comfort zone, and hungry to tackle big problems, then I am the one for you.
